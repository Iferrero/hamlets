William Berthomière is Social Geographer and Senior Scientist at the CNRS (Unit of research PASSAGES). He holds a PhD of Geography on « Israel and Immigration. The Jews from Former Soviet Union, actors of the territorial and identity stakes of Israel » from the University of Poitiers (2000) and in July 2012, he received a French accreditation to supervise Doctoral research. During the last years, he was a recipient of the Lavoisier Post-Doctoral Fellowship funded by the French Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Research fellow at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem and the French Research Center of Jerusalem. He also received the CNRS Bronze Medal in 2004. And he was the Director of Migrinter from October 2006 to March 2011 and co-director of the Revue Européenne des Migrations Internationales (REMI)

Since 2017, William Berthomière is Scientific Expert for the High Council for Evaluation of Research and Higher Education (HCERES).

His current scientific activities are devoted to mobilities and the international migrations in rural areas and more specifically oriented to: 1. the concepts of diaspora and migrant territoriality, 2. the notions of public space and presence. A large part of his fieldwork activities is developed in Mediterranean countries and Latin America. In terms of methodology, William Berthomière is also working on the use and the status of fieldwork photography in the scientific production in the social sciences.
